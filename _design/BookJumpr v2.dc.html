About

Books talk about books.

A character picks a paperback off a shelf. A narrator quotes a novel they loved at fifteen. A biography lists what its subject read on a long voyage. BookJumpr collects those moments — explicit mentions of one book inside another — and turns them into a network you can wander.

What counts as a mention

A book has to be named on the page: read, quoted, discussed, or dropped into conversation. Footnotes, bibliographies, and back-of-book references don’t count. Neither do allusions a critic might spot — if the text doesn’t say the title, it’s not in.

How the data works

The whole site is generated from two simple lists. One row per mention, one row per known book — every page, gallery, graph, and stat is derived from those.

mentions.csv  →  source book, mentioned book, mentioned author
books.csv    →  title, author, year, synopsis

Books without a row in books.csv still get a page — they just wait for a synopsis. Right now the network holds {{ st_sources }} indexed books and {{ st_links }} logged mentions.
